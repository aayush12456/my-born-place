Ujjain Tourism


"The Home of Mahakaleshwar"

ujjain considered to be one of the holiest cities of india is an ancient city situated on the eastern bank of the Shipra River in the Malwa region Madhya Pradesh.Ujjain is one of the four sites for the Khumbh Mela the largest peace time gathering on the planet that attract 100 million people to the festival.
This makes Ujjain an important place of Hindu Pilgrimage.To add to that it is also home to the Mahakaleshwar Jyotrilinga one of the twelve Jyotrilinga shrines to the god Shiva.Ujjain is one of the most glorious cities of ancient india as it was also known as educational hub of various indian scholars.The immense wealth of Ujjain in terms of religion architecture and educational value makes this is a top attraction amongst not only indian travellers but also amongst foreign tourist as well.Ujjain is located at a distance of 52 km from indore which is also the near major airport.

places to visit in Ujjain

1.Mahakaleshwar Jyotrilinga

Mahakaleshwar Temple is another temple in the state of Madhya pradesh which is dedicated to the largely revered God-Lord Shiva.it is one of the 12 jyotrilinga in india and is designed just as intricately as the other 11 are.

2.Kal Bhairava Temple

Bhairava is a fierce manifestation of Lord Shiva and Kal Bhairava is the most important among the eight Bhairavas.if ancient scriptures are to be believed,kal Bhairava temple is said to be related with the Tantra cult,a secret religious sect who trived upon black magic.

3.Ram Ghat

Ram Ghat is of immense religious significance to Hindus because it is one of the four locations where the Khumbh Mela takes place every 12 years.

4.Khumbh Mela

The khumbh mela is a sacred mass pilgrimage of the Hindus where devotees gather to take a dip in a holy river.The khumbh is held every three years in one of the four cities,Haridwar,Allahabad,Nashik,and Ujjain.

5.Harsiddhi Temple

The Harsiddhi Temple boasts of an idol of Annapurna painted in dark red colour between the idols of Mahahsaraswati and Mahalaxmi.

6.Kaliadeh palace

Situated on an island based in the Shipra river this Palace holds immense religious significance.it was constructed in 1458 AD.The palace is flanked on both sides by the water of the rivers and the technical aptitude of the predecessors can be seen in the man made tanks and channels

7.Jantar Mantar

Ujjain has always been an educational hub since ages now and this place just give us enough evidence as to why it is considered so jantar mantar (also called the vedh Shala Observatory) was established in the 17th century was established so as to help the Hindu scholars and astrologers with their research and studies.

8.chaubis Khamba Temple

Chaubis Khamba Temple is a captivating historical wonder dated back to the 9thor10thcentury.The entrance showcase the images of guardian goddesses of the Temple-Mahalaya and Mahamaya with their names inscribed on the footsteps of the temple

9.Sandipani Ashram

Being the very spot where Guru Sandipani coached Lord Krishna Balram and Sudama the Sandipani Ashram has many mentions in Mahabharat.The ashram houses a giant stone with 1-100 inscribed on it,as first written by Guru Sandipani.Be sure to check out the mystical Gomati Kunda which holds blend of all the holy waters in the world.

10.Isckon Temple

The beauty of the majestic white marble building of ISKCON in Ujjain is truly a sight for sore eyes The impeccably sculpted marble idols of Radha Madhana Mohan, Shri Krishna and Balaram and shri Gauri Nitai dressed in vibrantly coloured dresses and beautiful jewellery will definitely lift your spirits.Be sure to try some mouth-wathering dishes at Govindas,right next to ISKCON.

11.Kalidasa Academy

Spanning over a whopping 3374 hectares the kalidasa academy in ujjain has helped hundred of students get in touch with me world's first languages-Sanskrit.Right from translating ancient sanskrit script to the authentic reconstruction and production of Kalidasa's poems.this academy does it all Many fun filled and vivacious festivals are celebrated here like kalidasa festival Nandi and the kailash Yatra

How to reach Ujjain by flight

Nearest aerodrome is the indore Airport which is 55 km away from the city.Indore is connected to all major cities via air and it wouldn't be a problem to reach Ujjain from indore as the cabs are available in plenty

Nearest Airport

How to reach Ujjain by road

Ujjain is well connected by state road transport public bus services.Regular bus services are available from major cities of MP to Ujjain.super fast and Deluxe A/C buses are also available in these routes.Ujjain is very much traversable via road.


How to reach Ujjain by train

Ujjain Junction Railway Station is a major railway station itself and is connected to all the major stations of the country

Best Time to Visit Ujjain

What is the best time to visit Ujjain

October to March are the best months to visit Ujjain as the Weather is Pleasant and windy.it is the perfect time for sightseeing as the entire place looks spellbinding with temperatures hovering at a pleasant 20 degree celcius.During winters the whole town is enveloped in the mist when the mornings are chill and nightface frigid temperatures.Summers are comparatively torrid with temperatures reaching as high as 45-degree Celcius like other parts of MP.Hence Ujjain is best visited during Winters especially in the month of March when the Khumbh Mela kicks off once in every 12 years.
